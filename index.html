William Burroughs develops an adding machine.

Charles Herrold in San Jose starts the first radio station in the USA with regularly scheduled programming.

The first programmable computer, the Manchester Baby, runs the first program.

Atari announces the Atari 800, designed by Jay Miner.

Apple goes public for a record $1.3 billion.

Nintendo releases the Family Computer, renamed Nintendo Entertainment System in the USA.

Microsoft releases Windows 1.0 for MS-DOS.

Adobe releases Photoshop.

Stanford student Jerry Yang founds Yahoo.

The first DVD player is introduced by Toshiba.

Two Stanford students, Larry Page and Russian-born Sergey Brin, launch the search engine Google.

Mark Zuckerberg founds the social networking service Facebook at Harvard University (soon relocated to Palo Alto).
